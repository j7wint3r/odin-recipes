Pizza

Description

A Margherita pizza is a classic Italian pizza known for its simplicity and fresh, high-quality ingredients.

It typically features a thin, crispy crust topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and basil leaves, often drizzled with olive oil.

The colors of these toppings (red, white, and green) are said to represent the Italian flag.

Ingredients

  • For the base:
    • 300g strong bread flour
    • 1 tsp instant yeast
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • For the tomato sauce:
    • 100ml passata
    • handful fresh basil
    • 1 garlic clove
  • For the topping:
    • 125g ball mozzarella
    • handful grated or shaved parmesan
    • handful of cherry tomatoes
    • handful of basil leaves (optional)

Steps

  1. Make the Base:
    • Put the flour into a large bowl, stir in yeast and salt
    • Make a well, pour in 200ml warm water and olive oil
    • Mix with wooden spoon until soft, fairly wet dough forms
    • Turn onto a lightly floured surface, knead 5 mins until smooth
    • Cover with a tea towel and set aside
    • Optional: leave dough to rise for a thicker base
  2. Make the sauce:
    • Mix passata, basil, and crushed gralic together
    • Season to taste
    • Leave at room temperature while shaping the base
  3. Roll out the dough:
    • If risen, knead dough briefly
    • Split into two balls
    • Roll each ball on a floured surface into large rounds
    • Ensure dough is very thin
    • Place rounds on floured baking sheets
  4. Top and Bake:
    • Heat oven to 240°C / 220°C / gas 8
    • Place a baking sheet or upturned tray on top shelf to preheat
    • Spread sauce on bases with back of spoon
    • Place one pizza onto the hot tray in oven
    • Bake 8-10 mins until crisp
    • Serve with extra olive oil and basil leaves
    • Repeat for second pizza
