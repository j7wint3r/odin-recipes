Sisig

Description

Sisig is a well-loved, sizzling Filipino dish originating from Pampanga, Luzon, traditionally made from various parts of a pig's head like cheeks, ears, and snout, along with pork belly and chicken liver.

Ingredients

  • 1lb pork belly
  • 1 whole pork snout + jowls
  • 1 pig ear
  • 1/2 cup chicken liver
  • For boiling:
    • 1 cup vinegar
    • 1/2 cup soy sauce
    • 12 garlic cloves (1 head of garlics worth)
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 tablespoon salt
    • 1/2 tablespoon peppercorns
  • 1 sisig seasoning packet, or:
    • 1/4 cup kalamansi juice
    • 1/4 cup vinegar
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 tablespoon ground pepper
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 10 thai chilis, chopped
  • 3/4 chopped shallots
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 egg for topping
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Instructions

  1. Combine pork belly, face ears, vinegar, soy sauce in a stock pot. Fill with water until meat is covered. Add bay leaves,salt, and peppercorns Bring to a boil then simmer for at least an hour or until meat is no longer tight and tough.
  2. Remove meat from pot and grill for 10 minutes
  3. Chop pork belly and face meat into 1/4-1/2 inch cubes. Finely chop pig ear pieces
  4. Mix chopped pork in a bowl and add Thai chilis, kalamansi juice, vinegar, salt, pepper, and garlic powder (or seasoning packet)
  5. In a saute pan over medium heat, add 1 tablespoon oil and saute shallots for 1-2 minutes. Add chickenn livers; mash and saute until cooked thorough
  6. Add chopped pork to a saute pan, saute until meat is hot. Add mayonnaise and combine.
  7. (Optional) Heat a cast iron platter,add 1 tablespoon butter, add a serving of sisisg and top with a cracked egg while still hot
